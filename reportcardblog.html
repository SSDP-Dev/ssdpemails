MAKING SENSE

Southeast Region

  • University of South Florida SSDP co-hosted CannaDay Orlando Marijuana Conference and Expo with Regulate Florida on July 14th. The event was well attended by Florida Medical Marijuana patients, veterans, USF students, and members of the greater Orlando area.

Mid-Atlantic Region

  • The University of Pittsburgh SSDP and the Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies (MAPS) co-hosted the Sleeping Octopus Assembly on Psychedelics Conference, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, from July 13-15, 2018. This conference featured presentations from key figures in the psychedelic research and policy community, a psychedelic-themed comedy show, live musical performances, and a benefit dinner for MAPS.

Pacific Region

  • Occidental College SSDPers successfully expanded their school’s Good Samaritan Policy so that there are no longer special circumstances under which it couldn’t be applied

International

  • SSDP chapters in eight countries participated in this year’s 2018 Support Don’t Punish Global Day of Action. Some of the highlights include:
    • SSDP Nigeria organized marches and educational rallies in five different locations throughout the country and were joined by representatives of law enforcement and the Ministry of Health at the Abeokuta event.
    • SSSDP Sierra Leone held a day long conference attended by the Minister for Youth Affairs, who called it “the most outstanding, popular and successful drug policy program ever organized in Sierra Leone.”
    • EPSD México held an educational conference in México City that featured speeches on health and drug policy. Similar events were held in three other Mexican states.
    • For a full list of Support Don’t Highlights from our network, check out our recap blog post.

IN THE NEWS

  • Congressional Republicans have blocked at least 34 marijuana and drug policy amendmendments from being considered on the House floor during this Congress. As Chairman of the House Rules Committee, Congressman Pete Sessions (R-TX) is largely responsible for blocking these amendments and newly proposed bills.
  • A new study conducted by researchers from the University of California San Diego and Weill Cornell Medical College found state medical cannabis laws are associated with significant reductions in opioid prescriptions. "[If] all the states had legalized medical cannabis by 2014, Medicaid annual spending on opioid prescriptions would be reduced by 17.8 million dollars," the researchers projected. The study is being published in the journal Addiction.
  • NORML launched a letter-writing campaign to garner constitution support prior to the organization’s lobby day in D.C. July 23rd. You can still write your representatives and senators, take action!
  • New study undermines prohibitionists’ claims that legalization causes more youth marijuana use.
